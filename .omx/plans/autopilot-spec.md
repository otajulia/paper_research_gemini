# Autopilot Spec: CPF Tier and Healthcare Expenditure (2026-03-08)

## 1) Manuscript Core Positioning
- Current manuscript topic: association between comprehensive physical fitness tiers and 12-month healthcare expenditure using linked national administrative databases (NFA100 + NHIS) in Korea.
- Best-fit scientific fields:
  - Public health / population health policy
  - Health services research
  - Health economics / outcomes research (HEOR)
  - Aging and functional epidemiology

## 2) Venue Scan (designed with pre-2020 relevance in mind)

- The BMJ (high-impact general medicine with policy relevance)

## 4) Novelty Gap vs. Prior Evidence (up to 2020)
- Prior literature (pre-2020) strongly supports links between inactivity/functional decline and utilization/outcomes, but mostly not a nationally linked, objective, multi-component fitness-tier framework tied to claims costs at this scale.
- Current manuscript novelty candidate:
  - Objective and standardized national fitness battery + claims linkage
  - Multi-component tiering (health + skill/functional domains)
  - Older-adult mobility component effects on spending
- Remaining gap to top-tier acceptance:
  - Causal identification remains limited (association-heavy)
  - Selection/linkage bias quantification is not yet publication-grade for top-tier journals
  - Robustness/transportability/policy counterfactuals are not yet sufficiently extensive
